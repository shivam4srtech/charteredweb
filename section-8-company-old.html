What do I need to know about forming my LLC?

What is s Section 8 Company?

A Section 8 company is a type of business entity registered under the Companies Act 2013. Unlike a private limited company, which primarily aims to generate profits, a Section 8 company is established to promote non-profit objectives. These objectives can include:   Arts Commerce   Science   Research Education Sports Charity   Social welfare   Religion   Environmental protection.

Do I need a Section 8 Company?

A Section 8 company is particularly beneficial for organizations that prioritize social or charitable objectives over profit-making. While not mandatory for all non-profit entities, incorporating as a Section 8 company offers several advantages. These include tax exemptions, enhanced credibility, easier access to government licenses and permits, and the ability to enter into contracts.

What does Section 8 incorporation mean?

Incorporating a Section 8 company involves submitting the SPICe+ form to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, along with the necessary documents such as e-MOA, e-AOA, and a declaration from a CA/CS/lawyer. Once approved, this separate entity can be used for various purposes such as recording business expenses, obtaining government licenses and permits, entering into contracts, and pursuing non-profit objectives.

How easy is it to form a Section 8 Company?

Forming a Section 8 company is generally a straightforward process. While it may require some initial effort, the process is not overly complex. Key requirements include a clear understanding of the organization's non-profit objectives, a unique name, and a registered address.

FAQs

Here are some common questions we receive from our customers. If you have any additional questions, please don’t hesitate to contact us.

Who can register a Section 8 Company?

Any person or association of persons can apply for registration.

Can a Section 8 Company engage in profit-making activities?

Yes, a Section 8 Company can engage in profit-making activities, but any profits must be used to further its charitable objectives. It cannot distribute profits to its members.

Are there any restrictions on the use of profits by a Section 8 Company?

Yes, profits cannot be distributed to members. They must be used for the company's charitable purposes.

Can I use my residential address to incorporate a Private Limited Company?

At the time of Incorporation of a private limited company, the company needs to provide an address proof. The Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) allows a residential address to be used as the company’s registered address. Thus any address can be provided as the registered address

Is it mandatory for a Section 8 Company to have an audit?

Yes, it is mandatory for Section 8 Companies to have an audit.

What are the annual compliance requirements for a Section 8 Company?

Section 8 Companies must meet several annual compliance obligations, including filing financial statements and other relevant information with the Registrar of Companies, appointing an auditor to verify their financial records, holding annual general meetings to discuss company affairs, paying annual fees to the Registrar of Companies, and adhering to labor laws, environmental regulations, and other applicable legislation.

Section 8 Co vs Trust - What is better?

Choosing between a Section 8 Company and a Trust for your non-profit organization depends on your specific goals and needs. Section 8 Companies offer a more formal structure with greater transparency and accountability, while Trusts provide more flexibility in operations and governance. Factors to consider include the organization's purpose, legal requirements, tax implications, and desired level of control.

Section 8 Co vs Societies - Advantages?

Both Section 8 Companies and Societies offer distinct advantages for non-profit organizations. Section 8 Companies provide limited liability, legal recognition, and tax benefits, making them suitable for organizations seeking a more formal structure. Societies offer greater flexibility, simplicity, and lower costs, making them suitable for community-based organizations with less complex needs.
