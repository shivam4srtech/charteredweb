The types of ITR you need to file depends on your income sources and financial situation. There are seven types of Income Tax Return forms:

  • ITR-1 or Sahaj: For individuals with income of up to Rs.50 Lakhs.
  • ITR-2: For individuals and Hindu United Families (HUF) earning above Rs.50 Lakhs.
  • ITR-3: For Individuals and Hindu United Families from a business or profession.
  • ITR-4 or Sugam: For Individuals and HUFs having income from business and professions and opting for presumptive income scheme.
  • ITR-5: For partnerships firms and LLPs.
  • ITR-6: For companies
  • ITR-7: For trusts, religious institutions, or non-profit organizations.

Chartered ONE CA will determine the appropriate form for you based on your income, investments, and financial situation to ensure your return is filed correctly.

If in case you miss the ITR filing deadline, you may be subject to penalties (Rs 5,000) and interest charges. So, it is important to file your income tax return on time to avoid such consequences.

Yes, your Chartered ONE CA can review your past tax returns for missed deductions and if applicable guide you through the process of filming revised returns to claim those benefits.

The main difference between basic IT filing, multiple form 16 filing, and capital gains filing lies in income sources and complexity.

  • Basic ITR Filing is for resident individuals earning up to Rs 50 lakh from salary, one house property, and other sources like interest.
  • Form 16 Filing applies to individuals who have changed their jobs and need to file using multiple Form 16 from different employers.
  • Capital Gains Filing is for those who have earned or lost money from selling assets like shares, property, or jewellery.
