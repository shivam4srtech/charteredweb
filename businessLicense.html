About Private Limited Company Registration

In India, a private limited company (pvt ltd) is the most common and preferred business structure recognized under the Companies Act 2013. The primary objective of a pvt ltd co is to limit the personal liability of its owners. This means that the shareholders are only liable for the amount they have invested in the company and are not personally responsible for its debts or obligations.

The benefits of Pvt ltd co like limited liability protection, ease of formation and maintenance, and its status as a distinct legal entity make it the most favoured choice of business structure in India.

Private Limited Company vs Sole Proprietorship :
What's the difference?

There are several important differences between a Private Limited Company and Sole Proprietorship:

Pvt Ltd VS Proprietorship

Private Limited Company

Owned by shareholders

Private limited companies have shareholders, and their shares are privately held, meaning they cannot be traded on public stock exchanges.

Look more official

A private limited company are often seen as more credible, which can make it easier to do business with other companies.

Legal Status

A private limited company is a seperate legal entity. It can sue or be sued for its property & enter into legally binding contracts.

Accounting & Book-keeping

Companies Act, 2013 makes it mandatory for private limited companies to maintain their books of account at the registered office.

More appealing to investors

A private limited company can raise capital by issuing shares to investors, venture capitalists, or angel investors easily.

Pvt Ltd VS Proprietorship

Sole Proprietorship

Owned by Single Individual

A sole proprietorship is owned and operated by a single individual. It is not a seperate entity & does not have a seperate business PAN.

Informal structure

Appears less formal and professional due to its simple structure. It often operates under the owner's name or a trade name.

Legal Status

It is not a seperate legal entity from the owner. If the business is sued or cannot pay its debts, the owner's assets can be used to pay the debts.

Accounting & Book-keeping

Companies Act, 2013 makes it mandatory for private limited companies to maintain their books of account at the registered office.

Limited funding options

Investors cannot invest in sole proprietorship firm. Primarily reliant on personal savings, loans (secured against personal assets), or credit cards.

Private Limited Company vs Partnership Firm:
What's the difference?

There are several important differences between a Private Limited Company and a Partnership Firm:

PRIVATE LIMITED COMPANY VS PARTNERSHIP FIRM

Private Limited Company

Owned by shareholders

Private limited companies have shareholders, and their shares are privately held, meaning they cannot be traded on public stock exchanges.

Look more official

A private limited company are often seen as more credible, which can make it easier to do business with other companies.

Legal Status

A private limited company is a seperate legal entity. It can sue or be sued for its property & enter into legally binding contracts.

Accounting & Book-keeping

Companies Act, 2013 makes it mandatory for private limited companies to maintain their books of account at the registered office.

More appealing to investors

A private limited company can raise capital by issuing shares to investors, venture capitalists, or angel investors easily.

PRIVATE LIMITED COMPANY VS PARTNERSHIP FIRM

Partnership firm

Owned by Partners

Owned by two or more individuals, known as partners, who share profits and losses of the business in a predetermined ratio as specified in the deed.

Look more official

While generally less formal than a private limited company, a partnership can appear more official than a sole proprietorship, especially with multiple owners.

Legal Status

Not a separate legal entity and partners have unlimited liability. If the firm is sued or cannot pay its debts, the partners's assets can be used to pay the debts.

Accounting & Book-keeping

Exempt from mandatory book-keeping unless their income exceeds the prescribed threshold for tax audits under Section 44AB of the Income Tax Act.

Limited funding options

Investors cannot invest in a partnership firm unless they become partner of the firm. Primarily relies on personal capital & advances, loans, Cash Credits, etc.

Private Limited Company vs One Person Company:
What's the difference?

There are several important differences between private limited company and a one person company:

PRIVATE LIMITED COMPANY VS ONE PERSON COMPANY

Private Limited Company

Owned by shareholders

Private limited companies have shareholders, and their shares are privately held, meaning they cannot be traded on public stock exchanges.

Look more official

A private limited company are often seen as more credible, which can make it easier to do business with other companies.

Legal Status

A private limited company is a seperate legal entity. It can sue or be sued for its property & enter into legally binding contracts.

Accounting & Book-keeping

Companies Act, 2013 makes it mandatory for private limited companies to maintain their books of account at the registered office.

More appealing to investors

A private limited company can raise capital by issuing shares to investors, venture capitalists, or angel investors easily.

PRIVATE LIMITED COMPANY VS ONE PERSON COMPANY

One person company

Owned by Individual

An OPC is owned and controlled by a single person, who is both the shareholder and director.

Look more official

OPC offers a formal business structure, lending credibility and trust. which can make it easier to do business with other companies.

Legal Status

An OPC is a separate legal entity, distinct from its owner. It enjoys the same legal rights and obligations as a company.

Accounting & Book-keeping

Companies Act, 2013 makes it mandatory for one person companies to maintain their books of account at the registered office.

Limited funding options

To raise significant capital through equity, an OPC would typically need to convert to a private limited company.

Look more official

Owned by Shareholder
