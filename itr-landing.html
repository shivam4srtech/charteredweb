FAQs on ITR

An Income Tax Return (ITR) is a form used to declare your income, expenses, tax deductions, and tax paid during a financial year. Filing it is essential to report your financial status of the income to the Income Tax Department of India.

Anyone earning more than the basic exemption limit must file an ITR. For old tax regimes, this basic exemption limit is 2.5 lakh and for new tax regimes, this basic exemption limit is 3 lakhs. For old tax regimes, this basic exemption limit is 2.5 lakh and for new tax regimes, this basic exemption limit is 3 lakhs.

Filing Income Tax online with Chartered ONE is a simple process and can be done from the comfort of your home.

  1. Visit the Chartered ONE website and click on the “File ITR Now”.
  2. Select your source of income and proceed with the application process.
  3. If you're a salaried individual, upload your Form 16. If you don’t have Form 16, you can skip and continue.
  4. Enter details like the Financial Year, PAN Number, Aadhaar Number, Employment Details, Tax Deductions(if any) and Bank Details.
  5. Review your tax computation and click “File My ITR” to submit.

You can also opt for expert assistance from Chartered ONE’s CAs for accurate returns, maximum deductions, and tax refunds. The Income Tax e-fling portal also allows you to easily file your return online.

The due date for filing income tax returns is July 31 of the same year. However, this may change, so it's always best to check the latest deadline each year.

There are seven ITR forms for individuals (ITR 1, ITR 2, ITR 3, ITR 4, ITR 5, ITR 6 and ITR7). Each of these forms is meant for different taxpayers based on their income type such as salaried employees, business owners and trusts. You need to choose the correct form when filing.

If you missed the income tax filing due date, you can still file a late return. The belated return must be filed before the end of the assessment year. However, you might lose certain tax benefits and the ability to carry forward losses. For FY 2025-26, you may file the belated return by 31 December 2025.

To file ITR, you will need the following documents

  • Form 16 (for salaried individuals)
  • Aadhar and PAN Card
  • Salary Slips
  • Interest certificates from banks
  • TDS certificate issued by banks and others
  • Annual Information Statement
  • Proofs of tax-saving investment
  • Proofs of capital gains
  • Details of foreign assets and investments
  • Form 26AS

After submitting your ITR, you can check its status online using your PAN number on the Income Tax Department’s official website. Navigate the “View Returns/Forms” under the “My Account” section. Select the relevant assessment year and you will be able to see the status of your ITR whether it is processed, under review or pending.
