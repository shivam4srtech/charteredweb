Income Tax Notice - Types of ITR Notices & How To Authenticate Them

An income tax notice is an official letter issued by the Income Tax Department to inform a taxpayer about an issue with his tax account. This notice may be sent for different reasons including failure to file a return, errors in a filed tax return, or asking for additional details. Each type of ITR notice serves a different purpose and requires a different course of action.

Types of Income Tax Notices

The Income Tax Department sends following types of ITR notices to taxpayers, based on the specific issue.

Reasons for Receiving Income Tax Notice

The most common causes for receiving an income tax notice include:

  • Mismatch in the income reported and TDS details.
  • Error or discrepancies in your income tax return.
  • Missing or incomplete documents.
  • Failure to file or delay in filing your tax return.
  • Investing in your spouse‚Äôs name without mentioning it in your tax return.
  • Not reporting high-value transactions.
  • Incorrect details of income or assets.
  • Random scrutiny by the tax officer.
  • Failing to report long-term capital gains.
  • Using the wrong income tax return form.
  • Past tax invasion or outstanding tax liabilities.
  • Not paying self-assessment tax.

Steps After Receiving an Income Tax Notice

After receiving a notice from the Income Tax Department, it is important to approach the situation with care and order. Here‚Äôs what to do after receiving an income tax notice:

Step: 1

Read the Notice Thoroughly:

Go through the notice and find out the reason behind the notice, including what the Income Tax Department is asking for or pointing out.

Step: 2

Verify Your Details:

Confirm that the notice is for you by checking your name, PAN number, address, and the assessment year.

Step: 3

Figure Out the Issue:

Identify any problem such as income discrepancies, large transactions, or missing information in your tax return that caused a notice to be issued.

Step: 4

Respond to the ITR notice

Reply within the specified timeframe to avoid penalties or further legal issues.

Step: 5

Provide Necessary Documentation:

Provide accurate information in your response and include all relevant documents and explanations to clearly address the query or issue raised.

Step: 6

Check Your E-Filing Account

Check if the notice is also available in your online account on the Income Tax e-filing portal for verification and tracking purposes.

Step: 7

Get Expert Advice:

If you are unsure about the notice or how to deal with it, seek expert advice and guidance from a tax professional.

What are the Methods of Serving Income Tax Notices?

The Income Tax Act, 1961 specifies how notices, summons, or orders are delivered to ensure clear and legal communication with taxpayers. Notices are addressed to individuals, guardians of minors, or key representatives in groups and entities. Here is a breakdown of the methods for serving income tax notices:

Service by Post:

Notices can be sent using a registered post. The notice is considered served when sent to the correct address, whether received by taxpayer, an employee or an authorized person.

Service by Affixture:

If the recipient refuses to accept or cannot be located, the officer can affix a copy of the notice on a visible part of the recipient‚Äôs residence or business. A report is then filed stating the location of the notice.

Notices to HUFs and Partnership Firms:

Directed to the heads of Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), partners in firms, or members in associations to ensure collective responsibility.

Closed Businesses:

In case of a closed business, notices are served on the individual or company responsible for the income assessment. For firms the notice goes to any partner and for companies it is sent to the principal officer or director.

  • Proof of income such as salary slips or Form 16 (Part B).
  • TDS Certificates or Form 16 (Part A).
  • Investment proofs, if applicable

How To Authenticate Notice/Order Issued by ITD?

Before responding to an income tax notice, it is essential to authenticate it. You can authenticate a notice or order issued by the Income Tax Department by following these steps:

  • Step 1.Visit the official e-filing portal of the Income Tax Department click on ‚ÄúAuthenticate notice/order issued by ITD‚Äù usually found under the ‚ÄúQuick Links‚Äù section.
  • Step 2. To authenticate the notice select an authentication method:

Method 1: Using PAN and other details

  • Fill in all the details like PAN, assessment year, and document type.
  • Once the details are filled, enter the OTP received on your registered phone number.
  • After OTP validation, the DIN (Document Identification Number) and the issue date of the notice will be displayed.
  • If the notice is not from ITD, a message like ‚ÄòNo Record Found‚Äô will appear.

Method 2: Using DIN and Mobile Number.

  • Enter the Document Identification Number and your phone number.
  • An OTP will be sent to the registered mobile number. Enter the OTP in the designated field.
  • After entering the OTP, you can verify if the notice is genuine and a success message will confirm its authenticity.
