What do I need to know about forming my Private Limited Company?

What is a Private Limited Company?

A Private limited company (Pvt Ltd) is a type of business entity you can register that is recognized under the Companies Act 2013. The primary objective of a private limited company is to limit the owner's personal liability of its owners. This means that the shareholders are only liable for the amount they have invested in the company and are not personally responsible for its debts or obligations.

Do I need a Private Limited Company?

A private limited company isn't always required. Still, many small business owners choose to incorporate a private limited company to protect their personal assets if the company faces financial difficulties or legal issues. Having a private limited company can also help you to get DPIIT startup recognition, get tax benefits, enter into contracts, get funding, and get necessary government licenses and permits.

What does Pvt Ltd Company Incorporation mean?

When you incorporate a private limited company, you submit the SPICe+ form to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, along with SPICe e-MOA, e-AOA & CA/CS/Lawyer declaration. Once it's approved, you can use this separate entity to record business expenses, take on business debts, file taxes, obtain business licenses, and more‚Äîand this is what gives you liability protection.

How easy is it to form a private limited company?

You may be slightly intimidated by the idea of forming a legal entity like a private limited company, especially if it's your first time. All you need is an understanding of what your business will do, how you plan to run it, a unique name for your business, and an address for registration which can be your home address also.

LLC vs. corporation:
What's the difference?

There are several important differences between an LLC and a corporation:

LLC VS CORPORATION

LLC

Flexible management structure

LLC owners have more freedom to decide how the business is run and managed.

More taxation options

An LLC has flexibility in how it is taxed to help maximize savings.

Minimal record-keeping

An LLC does not need to record minutes or hold an annual shareholder meeting.

LLC VS CORPORATION

Corporation

Owned by shareholders

Corporations have shareholders instead of members, and they issue stock.

Look more official

Corporations are often seen as more credible, which can make it easier to do business with other companies.

More appealing to investors

A C corp, but not an S corp, can go public and issue stock to new investors, which makes it easier to raise revenue.
